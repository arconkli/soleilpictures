# Soleil Boards — Competitive Compendium (May 2026)

**Scope:** Every competitor we've researched across the six categories Soleil now plausibly touches: visual canvas/boards, adjacent whiteboards/design tools, cloud storage, all-in-one workspaces / PKM / docs, creative production verticals, and project management. Pricing and business model for each, plus the patterns and gaps to position into.

**Use this doc as:** a pricing-strategy reference, a competitive-positioning input, and a sanity check for any "we're like X" claim. Numbers are mostly 2026, sourced via the linked research agents — single-source aggregator data (Latka, fueler) should be treated as ±20-30%.

---

## Quick orientation: pricing has clustered into bands

After surveying ~100 products, paid-tier pricing has converged into seven gravity wells. Almost every SaaS-style competitor anchors at one of these:

| Band | Examples | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **$3-5 / mo** | Bear, Obsidian Sync, Logseq Sync, Craft Plus, Todoist, TickTick | Prosumer single-purpose (notes, todos, sync) |
| **$7-10 / user / mo** | Notion Plus ($10), Linear Basic ($10), Coda Pro ($10), ClickUp Unlimited ($7), Tana Plus ($8), Slab Startup ($6.67), Slite Standard ($8), Trello Standard ($5-10), Confluence Standard ($6.40), Jira Standard ($8), Affine Pro ($8) | Solo professional / very small team |
| **$10-15 / mo flat** | Milanote ($9.99 indiv), Reflect ($10), Mem ($12), Mymind ($12.99), Dropbox Plus ($11.99), Microsoft 365 Personal ($9.99), Notion AI add-on (formerly), Motion Pro ($13) | Individual prosumer with media/AI |
| **$15-25 / user / mo** | Notion Business ($20), Asana Advanced ($24.99), Monday Pro ($19), Wrike Business ($25), Confluence Premium ($12.30), ClickUp Business ($12), Slite Knowledge ($20), Mural Business ($20), FigJam Org ($5)+Figma ($16), Box Business ($20), Frame.io Pro ($15), Frame.io Team ($25), Air starter ($250 flat→$600), Dropbox Standard ($18), Sync.com Teams ($15) | Team/business with AI bundled |
| **$30-70 / user / mo** | Box Business Plus ($33) / Enterprise ($47), Wrike Apex ($60-80), Airtable Business ($45), Smartsheet Business ($32), Adobe CC All Apps ($55), CC Pro ($70), ClickUp "Everything AI" ($28), Microsoft Copilot ($30 add-on) | Enterprise standard, agency creative |
| **$49-99 / mo flat team** | Milanote Team ($49 flat, ~50 users), StudioBinder Indie ($85)/Pro ($127)/Agency ($229), Craft Team ($50 flat for 10), Basecamp Plus ($15/user), Filmustage ($59-199), Approval Studio ($42-125) | Team flat or vertical SaaS |
| **$199+ / mo flat team** | Basecamp Pro Unlimited ($299 flat), Filestage Starter ($199)/Business ($329), Brandfolder ($30K-100K+/yr), Bynder ($33K-200K+/yr) | All-in-one or enterprise vertical |

Two structural facts to internalize:

1. **The $7-12/user/mo entry band is the universal floor for credible SaaS.** Anything below reads as prosumer-toy. Anything above $15 requires AI or team features to justify.
2. **AI is now table-stakes at the $20 tier.** Notion bundled AI into Business in May 2025; Linear/Wrike/Smartsheet/AppFlowy/Slab/Confluence all followed. Per-seat AI add-ons (ClickUp Brain $9, Copilot $30) still exist but are increasingly disliked.

## Cross-cutting Patterns

### Free tier strategies (most → least generous)

1. **Free-forever full app** — Obsidian, Logseq, Affine (cloud caps), AppFlowy, Apple Freeform, Apple Notes
2. **Free with unlimited users but feature-capped** — ClickUp (60MB storage), Notion (most generous on collaborators), Coda (viewers free)
3. **Free with item caps** — Milanote (100 cards), Are.na (200 connections), Mymind (100-card trial), Mem (25 notes/mo), GitBook (1 user)
4. **Free with storage caps** — Mega (20GB), Google (15GB), Box (10GB), Dropbox (2GB), iCloud (5GB)
5. **No free tier / trial only** — Heptabase, Reflect, Roam, Sunsama, Tresorit, Slite, Things 3

### AI billing patterns

| Pattern | Examples |
|---|---|
| **Bundled into top paid tier** | Notion ($20 Business), Wrike, Smartsheet, AppFlowy Pro, Slab Business, Linear (beta) |
| **Bundled at all paid tiers** | Mem, Reflect, Capacities, Affine, Coda, Motion, Reclaim |
| **Separate per-seat add-on** | ClickUp Brain $9, Microsoft Copilot $30, GitBook AI Advanced $149/mo |
| **Credit-metered** | Tana (500/2K/5K), Monday ($0.01/credit), Asana AI Studio, Airtable, Craft, Notion Custom Agents |
| **None native** | Obsidian, Logseq, Roam, Anytype, Bear, Final Draft |

### Lifetime / one-time license patterns

Rare but distinctive. Mostly indie/OSS-adjacent brands using it to peel power-users off SaaS:

- **pCloud** — $199 (500GB) / $399 (2TB) / $1,190 (10TB)
- **Internxt** — $300-$600 lifetime, stackable to 100TB
- **Affine** — $499.99 Believer Lifetime (Pro for life + 1TB cloud)
- **Roam** — Believer $500/5yr (prepaid sub, not true lifetime)
- **Obsidian Catalyst** — $25 one-time (beta access)
- **Eagle** — $34.95 one-time perpetual desktop
- **Final Draft** — $249.99 perpetual
- **Things 3** — €50/10/20 across Apple platforms

The credibility signal these send is **anti-rent-seeking** — creative pros increasingly resent subscription-only models.

### Team / family pricing structures

Most products force per-seat. Notable exceptions worth modeling:

- **Milanote Team** — **$49/mo flat for ~50 users** — incredibly aggressive
- **Basecamp Pro Unlimited** — **$299/mo flat, unlimited users**
- **Craft Team** — **$50/mo flat for 10 users**
- **Notion Family** — n/a (no family plan)
- **iCloud+** — Family sharing built into 2TB+ tiers (6 members)
- **Dropbox Family** — $19.99/mo, 6 users

Family/small-studio plans are scarce. **A 2-5 seat "studio" SKU at flat $15-30/mo is wide open** in the canvas/doc category.

## Strategic Implications for Soleil Boards

### Where Soleil fits structurally

Soleil sits in an unusual position: **architecturally closer to Affine** (docs + canvas duality, ~$8-12 band, OSS-adjacent options) than to Notion (no canvas) or Milanote (no docs). It also lives in a **richer infrastructure stack** than peers — Yjs + PartyKit + pgvector embeddings + OpenAI tagging worker is materially more capable than what Milanote/Are.na/Mymind ship.

### Pricing recommendation (synthesizing all bands)

Based on where pricing has settled across 100+ competitors:

| Tier | Price | What's included | Anchor competitor |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free** | $0 | Unlimited boards/docs, unlimited collaborators, 5-10GB storage, 25MB max file, AI tagging on small caps | ClickUp Free / Notion Free |
| **Plus** | **$10/user/mo annual** ($12 monthly) | 500GB storage, unlimited file size up to 5GB, full AI tagging + semantic search, version history, public share | Notion Plus ($10), Milanote ($9.99), Linear Basic ($10), Dropbox Plus ($11.99) |
| **Studio (flat team)** | **$29/mo flat for 5 seats** annual ($39 monthly) | 2TB pooled storage, all Plus features for 5 users, white-label share links, custom domain | Filling the wide-open "small studio flat" gap that only Milanote ($49/50 users) and Craft ($50/10 users) touch |
| **Team** | **$20/user/mo annual** ($24 monthly, 5-seat min waived) | 5TB pooled, SSO, granular permissions, audit logs, AI synthesis features | Notion Business ($20), Asana Advanced ($24.99), Frame.io Team ($25) |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Compliance, custom limits, dedicated support, BYO model | Notion Enterprise / Linear Enterprise |
| **Lifetime "Founder" tier** | **$399 once** (limit 500) | Plus features for life, 1TB cloud, founder badge, roadmap input | Affine Believer ($499.99), pCloud lifetime ($399) |

Rationale:
- **The $10 entry** matches universal expectation and undercuts Notion barely while including AI (which Notion now requires $20 for).
- **The $29 flat studio tier** is the highest-leverage move — fills a real market gap, captures small creative shops at low CAC, and the 5-seat assumption pulls in invited collaborators who later become individual users.
- **Skipping the per-credit AI** model avoids Notion/ClickUp's confusion and signals product confidence.
- **The lifetime tier** as a limited-quantity launch promo creates urgency, captures the "I'll never pay SaaS forever" segment, and credentials the brand with creative pros who hate Adobe-style rent.
- **Avoid Frame.io / Adobe per-seat enterprise lanes** — wrong fit for indie team.

### What NOT to copy

- ❌ **Adobe's 100GB-on-a-$70-plan** storage joke — but DO exploit it as a positioning advantage
- ❌ **Monday's 3-seat minimum + scale-in-5s** — universally hated
- ❌ **Wrike's 5-seat minimum on Business** — same
- ❌ **GitBook's site-fee + per-user double meter** — confusing, expensive
- ❌ **Notion's 5MB file upload cap on free** — feels coercive even though it works
- ❌ **Per-credit AI billing** at retail prices — creates UX anxiety; bundle instead
- ❌ **ClickUp's $9 Brain + $28 Everything AI tier proliferation** — too many SKUs

### Where the biggest single opportunity sits

**The Adobe storage gap.** 41M paying Adobe subscribers get 100GB on $55-70/mo plans. Their actual creative output (PSD/AI/PRPROJ files) is gigabytes per project. They store the overflow in Dropbox/Drive — fragmented, painful, expensive.

A creative workspace that bundles **canvas + docs + 1-2TB storage + AI organization** at **$15-25/mo** is structurally compelling specifically to Adobe customers. With R2 zero-egress economics, this is gross-margin-positive at the unit level. No competitor offers this combination today (Air comes closest but is enterprise-priced).

**The wedge sentence:** "The workspace that makes you stop paying Adobe for storage and Dropbox for sharing."

### Where competitive density is highest (avoid)

- **Enterprise visual collab** — Miro ($665M ARR, $17.5B val) + Mural + FigJam own this; wrong product shape
- **Discovery/inspiration** — Cosmos ($21M raised, Apple App of 2025) owns the new wave; Pinterest at 619M MAU owns the casual lane; don't compete on discovery
- **Pure PM** — Linear, Asana, Monday, ClickUp, Notion are all $300M+ ARR; saturated
- **Pure DAM enterprise** — Brandfolder, Bynder, Air; need enterprise sales motion you don't have
- **Pure cloud storage** — Google, Dropbox, OneDrive; can't win on price or feature parity

### Where competitive density is LOWEST (target)

- **Creative-pro / prosumer canvas + docs + storage + AI** — nobody combines all four well
- **Small studio flat pricing** — Milanote and Craft are alone here
- **Film/TV pre-production canvas with script + breakdown + mood** — StudioBinder + Milanote + Filmustage do pieces; nothing unifies
- **The "Notion + Figma in one product" pitch** — Affine alone, and they're rough on UX/onboarding
- **Anti-Adobe storage positioning** — completely unowned

## Closing notes on data quality

- Public-company filings (Adobe, Figma, Asana, Monday, Smartsheet, Atlassian), government data (BLS, UNCTAD), and analyst-grade sources (Sacra, Crunchbase, primary founder interviews) are the most defensible.
- Aggregator data from getlatka, fueler.io, electroiq, sqmagazine — treat as ±20-50%. Triangulate with primary sources where stakes matter (pricing decisions, fundraising claims).
- 2026 market-size reports (Mordor, Verified Market Research, Grand View) are notorious for inflated and recycled figures — use only for directional CAGR, not absolute numbers.
- Smaller indie pricing (Mymind, Heptabase, Are.na, Capacities, Anytype) was verified against pricing pages directly during research.
